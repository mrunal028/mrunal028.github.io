Bio

I am a first-year Ph.D. student in the UNITES Lab at University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill, where I am fortunate to be advised by Prof. Tianlong Chen. Prior to UNC, I obtained an M.S. in ISysE at KAIST, advised by Prof. Chanyoung Park. During my Master's, I was honored with the Best Paper Award at the ICML WCB 2023 and spent wonderful time in Tokyo as a visiting researcher at Tokyo Tech, working with Prof. Tsuyoshi Murata and, Dr. Xin Liu.

My current research intersts ile in AI4Science and Multi-Agent LLMs. Specifically, I am interested in (A) addressing the inductive bias-key to the success-of LLMs, GNNs, and MoE with their (B) application in the field of Computational Biology. Additionally, I have recently been focusing on (C) communication strategies among Multi-Agent LLMs to achieve collaborative synergy.
